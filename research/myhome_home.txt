What Problems Does MyHome Help You Solve?

When you're searching for a property, knowing where to look is half the battle. On MyHome, you'll find every type of real estate you could need.



Choosing a location in Tbilisi 




Location in Tbilisi comes down to your priorities. Central neighborhoods like Vake and Saburtalo represent a stable investment, while growing outer districts like Didi Dighomi and Samgori offer more space for the money. Use MyHome's interactive map to find a home on exactly the street that fits your lifestyle.



Understanding prices 




To get a clear sense of property values across different Tbilisi neighborhoods, visit our analytics page. Price trends are fully transparent there, giving you a realistic picture of the market.



Budget rentals 




If you're looking for affordable housing, setting a maximum price in the filter is the most efficient approach. You'll only see listings that fit your financial situation, whether that's rentals in Tbilisi or other regions.



Holidays and rentals in Batumi 




For a leisure trip, the seafront strip and the New Boulevard are ideal. For a business visit, the old city center works well. Browse daily rental apartments in Batumi, where you can visually compare properties and book the one with the best view.



Selling or renting out quickly 




If you want to rent out or sell your property as fast as possible, MyHome is the best place to do it. Your listing is seen by thousands of potential interested parties every day, significantly increasing the chances of a successful deal.

How to Choose the Right Apartment




Start by defining your budget and your purpose. If you're buying as an investment, focus on rental potential. If it's for living in, prioritize infrastructure and transport links.



Newly built or older construction? 




New apartments come with modern standards and energy efficiency. Older buildings often have more solid construction and established neighborhoods around them.



City center or outer district? 




Buying in Vake or Saburtalo is prestigious, but in Didi Dighomi or Varketili, you could get significantly more space for the same budget.

Here's a practical example. If your goal is a long-term investment and your budget is $60,000, you could buy an under-construction apartment in Didi Dighomi - a white-frame unit of around 60 square meters. This makes sense because property values rise once construction is complete, and the developing infrastructure in that area ensures strong rental potential.




On the other hand, if you're planning to move in with your family right away and your budget stretches to $120,000, you might consider a finished older-build apartment in Saburtalo. While new builds are more energy-efficient, in this case, you'd be choosing a central neighborhood with the convenience that comes with it.
